The questions parents ask first

Is it a chatbot talking to my kid?

No. There’s no AI talking to your child, no auto-tutoring, no surveillance. It’s a game that teaches them about AI — they play, you stay in charge.

Does it collect my child’s data?

No. No account, no email, no servers. Everything runs on the device — the App Store privacy label reads “Data Not Collected.” We literally can’t see your kid.

Is it screen-heavy?

The opposite. Lessons are about five minutes — short, finite, purposeful, no autoplay. Designed to displace mindless scrolling, not add to it.

Is it secular?

Yes. It’s about understanding AI through evidence and critical thinking — no ideological pressure either way. Preview every bit of it yourself first.

What’s the catch with “free”?

There isn’t one. Nothing’s collected, so there’s nothing being monetized behind it. No subscription, no in-app purchases. Made in Canada.

Five minutes. No sign-up. If it’s not for you, delete it and no one’s the wiser.

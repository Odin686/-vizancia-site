A safer family AI routine.

AI tools can be useful study partners, but they are not parents, teachers, therapists, or a substitute for judgment. Use this checklist to make the safe choice the easy choice.

7 minute read•Reviewed July 14, 2026

1. Protect personal information

Teach a “minimum necessary” rule. Do not enter passwords, exact addresses, school schedules, financial details, health information, private images, or another person’s name and story without permission. Use a nickname or a fictional example when a real detail is not essential. Check whether a tool saves chats, trains on them, or shares them with service providers.

2. Verify before you rely

Language models can produce a fluent answer that is incomplete or wrong. For health, safety, legal, financial, or emotionally serious questions, stop and involve a qualified adult or professional. For schoolwork, compare important claims against a teacher-approved source, library resource, or primary document. Record the source and date so the answer can be revisited.

3. Keep a human boundary

A chatbot can imitate warmth, but it does not have feelings, confidentiality, or responsibility for a child’s wellbeing. Make a family rule that a child can always show a conversation to an adult without losing access to support. If a tool encourages secrecy, asks for intimate details, gives instructions for harm, or makes a child feel frightened or pressured, close it and tell a trusted adult.

Red flags: requests for private images or contact details; instructions to hide a conversation; pressure to act immediately; hateful or sexual content; advice that conflicts with a trusted adult; or certainty without evidence. These are reasons to pause, save only what is safe to save, and get human help.

4. Agree on a schoolwork protocol

Each school or teacher may set different rules. Decide together what is allowed: brainstorming, explaining a concept, creating practice questions, language feedback, or accessibility support. The learner should disclose AI assistance when required, keep their own voice, and be able to explain the final work. Never use a chatbot to impersonate a student or submit work the learner cannot defend.

5. Use age-appropriate settings

  • Choose a child or supervised mode when it is available, and review the provider’s age requirements.
  • Keep devices and accounts updated; use unique passwords and two-factor authentication for adult accounts.
  • Turn off optional history, personalization, or sharing features when they are not needed.
  • Set a time and place for AI use, with breaks and offline activities.

6. Report and recover

If an output is unsafe, report it through the product’s official channel and document the date and category without saving sensitive content. If personal information was shared, change affected passwords, tell a parent or guardian, and contact the service provider. If a child may be in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a trusted safeguarding professional.

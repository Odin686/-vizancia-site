Educational methodology

Teach the judgment, not just the tool.

Vizancia uses short cycles of explanation, practice, verification, reflection, and application so learners can build durable habits around AI.

01

Understand

Introduce one concept with plain language, familiar examples, and an explicit limitation before asking the learner to use or evaluate it.

02

Practise

Use retrieval, comparison, scenarios, and games so the learner must make a decision rather than simply recognize a definition.

03

Reflect

Ask what evidence would change the answer, who could be affected, what information should remain private, and when a person should take over.

From knowledge to action

Apply the idea and make the human contribution visible.

A learner should be able to explain the purpose, identify what AI did, describe what they verified or changed, and remain accountable for the finished result.

01

Explain in your own words

Distinguish a model's prediction from a verified fact.

02

Protect people and information

Recognize details that should remain private or require consent.

03

Verify what matters

Choose evidence appropriate to the claim, date, location, and consequence.

04

Own the final decision

Describe the human reasoning and know when not to use AI.

Age-aware progression

Independence should grow with maturity and evidence.

Child, teen, and adult modes can vary language, examples, complexity, and expected independence. Age alone does not determine readiness; supervision should also reflect the tool and the stakes.

Read the age-aware family guide

What improvement looks like

  • The learner can explain a concept without relying on buzzwords.
  • They can identify a claim that needs verification.
  • They choose a safer action when privacy or wellbeing is involved.
  • They can describe what the human contributed to a finished piece of work.

Reference framework and limitations

The progression is informed by the UNESCO AI competency framework for students. Vizancia adapts public principles to a family learning context and does not claim endorsement by UNESCO.

The methodology describes an educational design approach, not evidence that the product produces a particular learning outcome. Any future outcome claim should be based on an appropriate evaluation completed before the claim is published.

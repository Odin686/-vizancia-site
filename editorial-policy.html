Editorial standards

Useful, sourced, and honest about uncertainty.

This policy explains how Vizancia creates the AI Learning Hub, represents review status, handles AI-assisted drafting, and responds to corrections.

Current byline and review status

Resources are published under the organizational byline “Vizancia Incorporated” and reviewed through the internal process below. A named independent subject-matter reviewer is not currently represented on the site. Named reviewers and credentials will appear only after verification and authorization.

1. Accountability and review status

Each substantive resource displays a publication or review date and links to this policy. The organization is responsible for the final page. A byline, title, or credential is never added merely to make a page appear more authoritative.

When external clinical, legal, financial, safeguarding, accessibility, or educational expertise materially reviews a page, the contributor’s name, relevant role, scope of review, date, and any material conflict will be stated with permission.

2. Evidence and writing standards

  • Prefer original research, official public guidance, laws, policies, datasets, and direct technical documentation.
  • State the date, audience, geography, definitions, and important limitations of statistics.
  • Separate evidence from Vizancia’s interpretation and label recommendations as recommendations.
  • Do not publish fabricated testimonials, ratings, credentials, reviews, screenshots, quotations, citations, or product claims.
  • Avoid fear-based framing and do not imply that correlation proves causation.
  • Use age-aware language without treating age alone as proof of maturity, consent, or safety.

3. Research and review workflow

  1. Define the reader’s question and the consequence of an error.
  2. Collect primary and authoritative sources appropriate to the topic.
  3. Draft in plain language while preserving qualifications and uncertainty.
  4. Check every date, number, quotation, current policy, product claim, and link.
  5. Review age-appropriateness, privacy implications, accessibility, and internal consistency.
  6. Publish with a review date, correction route, and related resources.
  7. Revisit time-sensitive material when a source changes or a material correction is identified.

4. AI-assisted drafting

AI tools may assist with brainstorming, structure, or language editing. They are not an editorial authority or evidence source. A human must check the underlying source, wording, age-appropriateness, privacy implications, links, and final publication. Generated text is not presented as expert review.

5. Corrections and reader feedback

Email info@vizancia.ca with the page URL, disputed wording, and supporting source. Safety-critical concerns are prioritized. Material corrections update the review date and may be recorded in the changelog.

6. Sponsorships, affiliations, and conflicts

Paid placement, affiliate compensation, free products, commissioned research, or another material relationship would be disclosed on the relevant page. The current Learning Hub is educational content published by Vizancia and is not sponsored or endorsed by the sources it cites.

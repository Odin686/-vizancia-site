A repeatable homeschool rhythm

Learn one idea. Test it. Explain it back.

The strongest evidence of learning is not a completed screen—it is whether the learner can explain the idea, notice a limitation, and choose a safer next step.

01

02

Practise through a decision

03

Document the reasoning

Finish with a journal entry, discussion, source check, or portfolio note explaining what the human contributed.

Four-week starter plan

A practical sequence you can adapt.

Use the app for structured practice and the Learning Hub for discussion, writing, assessment, and offline extensions.

Week 1 · Explain

What AI is—and is not

Patterns, predictions, training data, and the difference between a generated answer and verified evidence.

Week 2 · Experiment

How prompts change output

Compare vague and specific questions while keeping personal information out of the activity.

Week 3 · Question

Bias, errors, and authorship

Identify an invented citation, a biased description, and work that hides the human contribution.

Week 4 · Create

A responsible final project

Build a source-backed explainer, quiz, or family AI policy and document every verification step.

Before you begin

Common homeschool questions

Current product details can change; the relevant store listing remains the source for compatibility and availability.

Is Vizancia a complete homeschool curriculum?

No. It is a supplementary AI-literacy app and resource library. Families remain responsible for local curriculum, assessment, reporting, and legal requirements.

Is the current app free?

Vizancia is free to download, and current lessons, games, and duels are included at no cost. Any future paid feature would be identified before purchase.

Can we use it without internet?

Lessons and most games work offline after installation. Multiplayer, leaderboards, platform services, and the AI Sandbox require a connection.

Does it replace adult supervision?

No. Adult involvement should increase with the learner's age, maturity, and the stakes of the topic. Connected AI practice is offered only through an adult-managed limited beta.

How do we document learning?

Use the four-part rubric in the lesson plan: explain a concept, protect private information, question a claim, and make the human contribution visible.

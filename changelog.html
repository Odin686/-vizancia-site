Product changelog

What changed, in plain language.

Not every code change appears here. This log highlights user-facing product, privacy, accessibility, marketing, and learning-resource updates.

UpdatesJuly 16 · Guided programsJuly 16 · iOS 3.5 claimsJuly 14 · Professional siteJuly 14 · Learning HubMay 31 · Store choice

July 16, 2026 · Guided AI literacy programs

  • Added a Programs page for workplace AI literacy, a web-only Builder Incubator, family safety, homeschool curriculum, and private coaching.
  • Made Programs & Services prominent in the homepage navigation, first-screen actions, and a dedicated homepage offer panel.
  • Published introductory prices of CA$750 for a fixed-fee workplace audit, CA$1,250 for a full-day workshop, CA$125 for private family or coaching sessions, and a planned CA$39 homeschool pack.
  • Added an email-based enquiry form that prepares a message to info@vizancia.com without uploading or storing form answers on the website.
  • Clarified that enquiries are not bookings, the homeschool pack is planned rather than currently sold, Builder Incubator dates and pricing remain to be confirmed, and guided education does not replace legal, security, or compliance specialists.

July 16, 2026 · iOS 3.5 product-claim reconciliation

  • Updated the iOS release facts to 16 categories, 96 lessons, 580 authored questions, 17 mini-games, 12 levels, and 28 achievements.
  • Replaced retired game names with current 3.5 titles, including AI Startup, Neural Assembly, Circuit Slide, and Self-Driving Sprint.
  • Aligned the parent page with four grade-band choices plus Adult / Lifelong Learner while narrowing the audience promise to learners 10+ and explaining what the setting actually changes.
  • Clarified that an optional first name or nickname stays on the device, student profiles cannot access global Game Center social features, and the online AI Sandbox is a website-only beta rather than part of the offline iOS app.
  • Scoped 3.5 claims to iPhone and iPad because Android release facts may differ, and retained store-version language for App Store processing and rollout delays.

July 14, 2026 · Professional site and trust update

  • Rebuilt the homepage and homeschool experience around a shared professional design system, sample lessons, curriculum proof, and explicit store choice.
  • Qualified privacy, offline, price, and Canadian-company claims so connected features are not hidden behind an absolute promise.
  • Added the AI Sandbox Safety and Acceptable Use Policy, adult-managed beta boundary, reporting guidance, and expanded AI terms.
  • Added age-aware AI literacy, verification, family agreement, and AI hallucination guides to the Learning Hub.
  • Improved small-text contrast, structured data, social metadata, keyboard fallbacks, and mobile layouts.
  • Added Google Ads conversion-event plumbing. Conversion destinations still require the labels created inside the Google Ads account.

July 14, 2026 · Website trust and Learning Hub

  • Added parent, homeschool, safety, glossary, research, and printable worksheet resources.
  • Added About, Educational Methodology, Editorial Policy, Accessibility, and Product Changelog pages.
  • Added a custom 404 page and reduced-motion safeguards.
  • Clarified the difference between the offline-first core app, optional connected features, and consent-controlled website measurement.

May 31, 2026 · Store choice and consent foundations

  • Made Apple and Google Play links explicit so visitors can choose their store on desktop or mobile.
  • Added consent-controlled Google Ads measurement and a persistent privacy choices control.
  • Removed unverified rating summaries and testimonials from landing-page copy.

For app release notes and current platform compatibility, review the relevant app-store listing. To report a missing user-facing change, email info@vizancia.ca.
